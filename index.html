Why Decisions Fail

Most decisions don't fail because people are wrong. They fail because the process is broken.

Anchoring Bias

The first idea anchors the room. Every subsequent thought orbits around it, limiting exploration.

Status Bias

Senior voices override insight. Hierarchy replaces merit in determining which ideas survive.

Conformity Pressure

Dissent disappears quietly. Teams converge on comfortable agreement rather than optimal outcomes.

Unexamined Risks

Urgency crowds out diligence. Risks go unexamined until they become consequences.

Organizational Amnesia

Context is lost weeks later. Teams repeat mistakes because rationale was never preserved.

How It Works

A structured system for unstructured thinking. Wigwam runs decisions through a phase-locked workflow designed to eliminate bias and preserve insight.

1

Solo Build Isolation

Participants think in isolation. No influence. No hierarchy. Just focused, independent reasoning before any exposure to group dynamics.

2

Reveal

All ideas appear simultaneously. No first-mover advantage. No anchoring. Every perspective enters the discussion with equal weight.

3

Analysis AI-Powered

AI clusters related ideas, identifies contradictions, and surfaces blind spots. Patterns emerge that humans alone would miss.

4

Synthesis

Multiple decision paths are generated with clear tradeoffs. Not consensus by exhaustion, but explicit choices with documented reasoning.

5

Decision Lock

The final decision is captured with its tradeoffs, dissenting views, and ownership. An immutable record for future accountability.

6

Reflection

Learning without ego. Teams review outcomes against original reasoning, building institutional wisdom over time.

The value isn't the meeting. It's the enforced thinking.
